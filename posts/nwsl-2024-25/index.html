The Growth of the NWSL

professional soccer
nwsl
women's soccer
Author

Alana Textor

Published

December 4, 2025

The National Women’s Soccer League (NWSL) is one of the fastest-growing leagues internationally. What began in 2012 with eight teams, has grown to 14, with plans to add two more teams in the cities of Boston and Denver.

The ultimate goal for every team in the league is to win the Championship. This is a major accomplishment and an achievement worth celebrating. In 2024 and 2025, two different teams earned the honor and what a better way to analyze the winning teams than with this data?

The league’s viewership reached an all-time high in the 2025 season, with an 18 percent in contrast to the 2024 season. However, although women’s sports are on the rise, the attendance for some NWSL teams have decreased over the past year.

Teams such as Angel City and the San Diego Wave, both based in California, experienced a significant drop in attendance. This could be due to several factors such as the traffic, the retirement of notable players such as Alex Morgan at the end of the 2024 season, etc.

Other teams with a decrease in attendance include the Utah Royals and Racing Louisville. The factors behind the decline are endless, as the Royals underwent a coaching change within the past year and Racing Louisville, is still working to find its footing, with 2025 marking its fourth season in the league.

Despite these challenges, the teams continue to defy the statistics against them while having large and supported fan bases.

There is a clear contrast between the 2024 and 2025 NWSL Champions.

In 2024, many predicted that the Orlando Pride would win the Championship. They finished their regular season in first place with 60 points and featured several standout players, including Marta and Barbra Banda.

In 2025, Gotham FC, the underdogs, won the championship for the second time in three years. Gotham finished the regular season with 36 points, seven places behind the first seed and the expected favorite to win, the KC Current, with 65 points.

The top goal scorers for Gotham FC and the Orlando Pride in the 2024 and 2025 seasons remained the same two players. For Gotham, Spanish national team striker Esther Gonzalez recorded nine goals in 2024 and increased her total to 13 in the 2025 season. Orlando’s Zambian national team striker Barbra Banda led the team with 13 goals in 2024 and eight goals in 2025. Both of these strikers are highly valuable to their clubs, with Gonzalez receiving a contract extension and Banda signing a four-year deal

The NWSL will continue to be on the rise and add highly-valuable players and investors to its league. Women’s sports has evolved over the past five years, allowing more growth within the league and its teams.
